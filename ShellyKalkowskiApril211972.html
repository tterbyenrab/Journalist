Armstrong Advocates Men's Lib


By Shelly Kalkowski, April 21, 1972

Equal rights for women are fine, Charles Armstrong thinks men need to be liberated too.

“If men can be chauvinists, then so can women,” he said. Armstrong is a former graduate student at the University of Nebraska Lincoln and has done work towards a master's thesis on men's liberation.

A key element of female chauvinism, according to Armstrong, is the expectation that the man must be the bread winner.

Other things men need to liberated from are the job stereotypes men are classified under and the idea that a "man" should not openly express his feelings, he said.

"Women are jumping from the frying pan into the fire when they ask for equal rights,” Armstrong said. “They are going from sexual oppression to economic oppression.”

The solution, Armstrong said he believes, lies in a guaranteed income that would free men, and women, from economic coercion.

"Men also need to be liberated from the stereotypes of jock, super-stud and unfeeling machine,” he said.

Armstrong said an Army manual that said an officer should not be seen carrying a baby was a good example of this stereotyping.

"Many people see a man as some sort of machine--an unfeeling robot-- that is not supposed to show any human feelings, such as crying,” Armstrong said. “I see men's liberation as trying to free yourself from the stereotype being imposed upon you.”

"Maybe what we need is equal rights for chauvinists."

Annotations

Shelly Kalkowski is an alumni of The University of Nebraska-Lincoln.
